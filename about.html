JR
The Story

It was 2020. The world had shut down, and I was sitting at my kitchen table trying to log my five-year-old into his online kindergarten class. The login information was on the counter — four feet away. I couldn't move.

Not "stuck" in the philosophical sense. Literally frozen. My body had decided it was done. What a therapist later described as a Psychogenic Non-Epileptic Event — a neurological shutdown caused by overwhelming psychological stress. My body's check engine light, finally forced on.

I had spent years doing what high performers are praised for doing: working harder, pushing longer, carrying more. From the outside, everything looked successful. Underneath, something was failing.

"I wasn't burned out because I lacked passion. I burned out because I lacked systems. That distinction changed everything."

— Jason Rumbough

That realization became The Bonfire Method — a framework for building fires that last. Not through more discipline or more passion, but through the systems that sustain both. Spark. S.Y.S.T.E.M.S. AIR.

Today Jason works in enterprise sales at Flow Control Group while simultaneously building The Bonfire Method as a book, coaching platform, and consulting framework. The goal: impact one million leaders over 15 years.

He lives in Nashville, Tennessee with his family. He gets up at 5am, trains hard, and believes — with full seriousness — that sustainable impact requires sustainable leaders.
